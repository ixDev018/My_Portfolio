Work Experience

Multimedia Creative Officer
SPCC Caloocan | CIT Department · 2022 - 2025

The Freshman Spark

When I first enrolled in the BSIT program at SPCC Caloocan, I was just a hobbyist. Video editing and graphic design were things I did purely on the side—documenting family memories or creating random visual art to accompany my music. But during the opening weeks of my freshman year, one of my professors, Engr. Rechelle Teves, actively looked for student creators with a knack for multimedia to help promote our department. I took the leap and accepted it as an extracurricular role. She saw raw potential in my hobbyist work and officially brought me on as a videographer.

Mentorship and Purpose

From that point forward, Miss Teves became my mentor. Her guidance gave me a deep sense of purpose in my role, transforming my perspective from someone who simply spliced clips together as a side hobby into a creator who understood the weight and impact of visual communication.

Evolution Within Limitations

Embracing the Intentional Craft

Starting out, I had to work incredibly hard to self-teach advanced cinematic concepts and navigate a steep learning curve across various production software. Operating within major technical limitations at the time forced me to innovate. It was during this intense learning curve that I unlocked my core creative philosophy: intentional creative work is what truly connects with an audience. A video shouldn't just be a collection of flashy clips; it must meticulously deliver the exact emotional response the viewer is supposed to feel the moment they watch it.

A Three-Year Legacy

Over a dedicated three-year tenure, I scaled the ranks within our department's creative pipeline. I evolved from a foundational videographer, animator, film editor, and video editor into a graphic designer, ultimately being appointed as the College of Information Technology Multimedia's Head Editor.

"A creator's worth isn't defined by the perfection of their gear, but by the intentionality behind every frame they render."

Organizational Roster & Leadership

Throughout my academic journey, I served as the creative backbone for our department's shifting student leadership bodies, executing diverse roles across consecutive council eras:

•
CITSO (College of Information Technology Student Organization 2022–2023): Served as the core Videographer and Editor during my foundational year.
•
CITE (Council of Information Technology Excellence 2024): Stepped up as a Videographer, Video Editor, Animator, and Head Director to elevate event trailers.
•
ITSA (Information Technology Student Association 2024–2025): Formally took the reins as Videographer, Video Editor, Animator, Graphic Designer, and Head Editor.

Freelance: Turning Skills into Solutions
Web Development, 3D & 2D Animation, Video Editing, Graphic Design · 2024 - Present · Present

The Bridge to Client-Based Work

Freelancing became a reality for me the moment I realized that the creative and technical skills I was refining could serve as a direct bridge to solving real-world client problems. What started as personal hobbies and internal school projects quickly evolved into an independent service pipeline, helping clients bring complex, high-stakes academic and professional requirements to life.

My Freelance Pillars

1. High-Pressure 3D Modeling & Animation

My entry into the freelance space began by providing 3D modeling and animation services for students within my department who needed to complete their complex finals projects. Because these clients typically handed over their requirements as high-stress, late-stage "rush" orders, I had to be incredibly disciplined—frequently engineering, texturing, and rendering complete projects in a week or less before their absolute deadlines. I handle all 3D modeling, animation, and creative material production strictly as an independent, solo creator.

2. UI/UX & Frontend Web Architecture

My second freelance pillar focuses on web-based clients looking to build custom systems from scratch or fix broken functional architectures. My responsibilities span the entire frontend pipeline: translating raw user requirements into initial structural wireframes, designing high-fidelity interactive prototypes, and occasionally jumping into the source code to engineer the live frontend interface.

"Whether working solo against a tight 3D rendering deadline or syncing with a development team on a web app, the objective remains the same: deliver production-grade quality under pressure."

Target Audience & Collaborative Dynamics

My freelance operations change dynamically depending on the scope and technology stack of the project at hand. Most of my contracts center around critical final capstone projects, serving clients both internally within my home campus and externally across other academic institutions.

•
The Client Base: Primarily students from Systems Plus Computer College (SPCC) Caloocan, alongside outside clients from other schools executing their final capstone projects.
•
Solo Execution: I operate completely independently when handling 3D modeling, animation assets, and promotional creative materials.
•
Team Deployment: When a project requires a complex web application framework, I scale my operations and collaborate directly alongside my startup partners at ODDS Inc. to deliver full-stack execution.

The Final Practicum Frontier

From mid-January to late March 2026, I completed my official On-the-Job Training (OJT) internship at the Multimedia and Communications Department of Systems Plus Computer College (SPCC) Caloocan. Far from a basic academic requirement, this 10-week journey was a high-stakes, collaborative operation where we functioned as an elite technical and creative unit. (insert impostor syndrome)

The Core Crew & Structure

"True full-stack execution means balancing software architecture with production-grade multimedia."

Instead of working in isolated silos, we structured our final practicum execution with clear roles, moving away from rigid Agile Scrum frameworks to focus on rapid, adaptive deployments.

•
The Development & Design Lead: Curated by me (Brix Jorie F. Cura), driving the technical direction, UI/UX, and Assisted by Sherwin T. Ramirez (UI/UX Assistant).
•
The Engineering Partners: Executed alongside my core technical teammates Kurt Jazam S. Laranio (Developer) and Robert L. Santiago Jr. (Developer).
•
The Academic Oversight: Guided directly under the supervision of our MCD Head, Mr. Randelio Belmonte.

Major Accomplishment — SPCC Website Modernization

Our definitive technical milestone during this 10-week tenure was the comprehensive modernization of the SPCC website, actively enhancing institutional systems across the Basic Education and College tiers.

•
Full-Stack Engine: Engineered the platform's backend infrastructure using Laravel, coupled with Tailwind CSS to build a highly responsive, custom-styled frontend utility layout.
•
The SPCC Bugle Integration: Designed the layout and digital framework for the school’s newspaper-style news portal built directly into the modernized site.
•
Modern Visual Polish: Implemented a high-end, high-contrast UI featuring deep Navy and Gold branding accents complemented by custom layout web components.
